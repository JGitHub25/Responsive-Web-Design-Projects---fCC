Julio Garavito: From Colombia to the Moon

In addition to being an astronomer, Garavito was also interested in Economics.

  • 1865 - Julio was born.
  • 1884 - He finished high school with and emphasis in philosophy and literature.
  • 1885-1887 - He had financial struggles but he didn't stop working in his research projects.
  • 1890 - He published his first paper.
  • 1891 - He graduated from the Engineering program with the thesis "El juego de la aguja" ('The game of the needle').
  • 1892 - He was appointed director of the National Observatory.
  • 1893 - He got married to María Luisa Cadena.
  • 1901 - Through the observation of a comet, Julio started working on celestial mechanics with the Hill-Brown and the Olders methods.
  • 1919 - The government of Colombia published all his works as a way to honor his memory.
  • 1970 - The National Astronomical Union named a crater in the dark side of the Moon after him.
